Account Driver vs Selfplay — Which is Better?

A detailed breakdown of both boosting options to help you choose the right one for your situation.

Updated March 2026 5 min read

When ordering a boost for The Finals, you'll have to choose between two service types: Account Driver and Selfplay. Both get you to your target rank — but the experience is completely different. Here's everything you need to know to pick the right one.

The Short Answer

🚗

Choose Account Driver if...

You want the fastest possible result with zero effort. Your account is Steam-linked. You don't mind sharing your login temporarily.

🎮

Choose Selfplay if...

Your account isn't Steam-linked. You never want to share your credentials. You want to learn while climbing.

Account Driver — Deep Dive

Account Driver is our flagship service and the choice of most customers who just want results. You hand us temporary access, we climb, and we hand it back.

The key advantage is speed. Since our boosters can play at any time of day — including through the night — Account Driver orders are often completed in a single day. There's no scheduling required on your end.

The main concern people have is account security. We take this seriously: all credentials are treated with complete confidentiality, we use a VPN matching your region, and we never use any third-party software. In 77 completed orders we have had zero security incidents.

Selfplay — Deep Dive

Selfplay is perfect for players without a Steam-linked account and anyone who prefers to stay in control. You queue with two of our top-ranked players who act as your carry teammates for every match. You play your own character — they handle the heavy lifting.

The experience is genuinely educational. Watching high-level players navigate cashout fights and rotations in real time is some of the best practical coaching available. Many customers improve their own gameplay significantly just from the sessions.

What Do Most People Choose?

Account Driver is chosen by roughly 70% of our customers — primarily because it's faster and requires no effort. Selfplay is the preferred choice for players without a Steam-linked account and anyone who prefers to stay actively involved in their games.

Both services are priced identically, so price should never be a deciding factor.
